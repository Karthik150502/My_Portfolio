Academic Project

Development of Industrial Pipe Inspection Robot

Robotics is a exponentially growing industry. It is employed to reduce the Human involvement where the task to be performed is risky and complex. Talking about risky tasks and regular inspections, one such industry where the tasks are risky and regular inspections are required is industries where inflammable fluids are being moved. The frequent supervision of Industrial pipes is necessary to prevent any uninvited accidents and failures, subsequently the plant efficiency is improved. Some industrial pipes are complex to inspect because of it's diameter constraint. Therefore, we employ an Industrial Pipe Inspection robot to enter those pipes and scrutinize.Any device to be able to perform ideally demands frequent inspection and maintenance. Industries where there is frequent circulation of fluids basically demand optimum pipe conditions. Of course, some pipes large in diameter and placed horizontally can be inspected by Humans. But, there may be instances where the pipe cannot be inspected by a Human, considering it being too long and its diameter being too small making it impossible to fit in and inspect, there also maybe complex pipe builds which includes L-Bends and elbows which is also a constraint. In some Gas and Oil Industries, it becomes a crucial procedure to inspect the pipes carrying flammable fluids frequently, very minute defects in those pipes can cause leaking of such fluids resulting in catastrophic accidents.Therefore, using any type of device that can perfectly fit inside smaller pipes and inspect is the ideal bet. And hence, using a mobile and agile Industrial pipe inspection robot which can travel inside pipes of smaller diameter to inspect and clean them sounds more convincing, we are therefore committed to build one which serves the purpose mentioned above. The picture beside is the 3D CAD model of the pipe inspection robot we are working on, which has not yet been fabricated and made to be fully functional.
